National Parks FAQ

Are pants required in all parks?
Absolutely. For goodness sake, have some consideration for the people around you.
If I scream, will people hear me?
Many of our parks have areas where screaming will not be heard. If we can hear you, please shut up.
Will park rangers bring me ice cream if it is an emergency?
If you call us, wanting ice cream, we will cut you.
How many visitors to the parks, per year, are aliens posing as human?
Pretty much all of them.
Is being handsome discouraged at the parks?
Physical attractiveness is neither encouraged nor discouraged. You do you.
My uncle was tackled and ejected from a park for using an air horn at night. Why so hateful?
We really do hate your uncle. He is not welcome here, and you probably aren't either.
Is it true that--
No. It's not.
If Superman and Bugs Bunny got into a fight, who would win?
Bugs Bunny, simply because he lives in a world without rules.
Can I ask another question?
No.
If I got lost, would I be rescued?
It depends on whether or not your uncle was the air horn guy.

Random Park Facts

Big Bend

  • Established in 1944.
  • Included the land from Big Bend State Park (established in 1933) that was deeded to the federal government for the park by the state of Texas.
  • Largest protected area of the Chihuahuan Desert.
  • There are more than 1,200 different plant species found in Big Bend National Park. Many are not found anywhere else on the planet.
  • There are more species of birds found in Big Bend National Park than in any other national park in the U.S. There are over 450 different species.

Yellowstone

  • Established in 1872, becoming the world's first national park.
  • Sits atop an active volcano.
  • Around four million visits each year. More than half of these visits happen during June, July, and August.
  • 1000-3000 earthquakes annually.
  • Over 500 active geysers (more than half of the world's total)

Olympic

  • Established in 1938.
  • Home to a rare temperate rainforest, the Hoh, which receives 12 feet of rain, annually.
  • Lake Crescent's waters lack nitrogen, so algae doesn't grow, and makes them crystal clear.
  • Banana slugs!
